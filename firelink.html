The Crisis

WHEN NETWORKS
FAIL, PEOPLE DIE

In the 2018 California Camp Fire, 17 cell towers burned down — cutting thousands off from 911 services at the exact moment they needed help most.

During the 2023 Maui wildfires, hundreds of 911 calls went unanswered because dispatch systems were completely overwhelmed.

Civilians trapped in fires have no reliable way to call for help. Firefighters enter buildings with no real-time intelligence on victim locations. The technology gap costs lives — and it's getting worse.

The Device

MEET R.E.A.C.H.

  • 📡
    Dual-Network: LTE + RF Radio
    Uses Motorola's APX Series Radio Line with a backup LTE connection — so it works even when cell towers fail.
  • 🎙️
    AI Voice Detection & Clarity
    Auto-detects human speech using NVIDIA Jetson Nano AI. Sends real-time location alerts to firefighter radios even when victims can't speak.
  • 🔥
    Fire-Resistant Housing
    Built with UL 94 V-0 polycarbonate/ABS blend — the same fire-resistant material used in Motorola radios.
  • 📍
    Real-Time Location Tracking
    Allows first responders to pinpoint victims by floor and zone, dramatically cutting rescue response times.
  • ⚡
    ESP32-Based Hardware
    Transmitter and receiver built on ESP32 with ESP-NOW protocol. Microphone module activates on fire alarm trigger — no human action required.
The Process

HOW IT WORKS

1
Fire Alarm Activates
When a fire alarm is triggered, R.E.A.C.H. units throughout the building switch on automatically.
2
AI Detects Voice
Onboard AI listens for human sounds. If a trapped civilian is detected, an automated alert is sent immediately.
3
Firefighters Notified
"Human activity detected on 3rd floor stairwell." Real-time alerts reach first responder radios via APX Radio System.
4
Two-Way Communication
Civilians can speak directly with firefighters. APX Noise Suppression System ensures clear audio through smoke and chaos.
